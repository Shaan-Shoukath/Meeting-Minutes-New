## Discussions & Key Decisions

### 1. Core Narrative & Audience Outcomes
The team refined the narrative framework to deliver specific outcomes for different audience groups:
- **Primary Audience (Makers):** Providing visibility, credibility, and network expansion.
- **Secondary Audience (Community Members):** Offers networking, skill workshops, and collaborative opportunities.
- **Tertiary Audience (General Public):** Spark curiosity, showcase hands-on making, and invite future participation.

### 2. Event Timeline & Sponsorship Constraints
The team discussed the prior proposal to shift the event timeline to January.
- *Jazeel* noted that companies allocate CSR budgets in Q3, which may impact our sponsorship drive if we extend the timeline too early.
- *Shan OG* advised against modifying timelines based on predicted challenges. The committee resolved to maintain the current execution timeline, collect actual sponsor commitments, and defer extensions until hard constraints require them.

### 3. Community-Led Narrative Development
To ensure the marketing matches community interests, the team will run informal interviews:
- Key questions to answer:
  - *Why are we running Maker Faire Kochi?*
  - *Why does this edition matter?*
  - *Who is it for, and what makes it unique?*
- The communication strategy will answer these questions directly.

### 4. Maker-Centric Storytelling
Rather than standard bios, maker registration and onboarding will focus on storytelling:
- Highlighting their personal maker journey, design challenges, open source contributions, and motivation.
